Book Summaries

Key lessons from self-improvement books — summarized by a student, for students.

Atomic Habits

by James Clear

★★★★★

The book that started this project. James Clear reveals how tiny changes compound into remarkable results.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • The Four Laws: Make it Obvious, Attractive, Easy, and Satisfying.
  • Two-Minute Rule: Shrink any habit until it takes less than two minutes.
  • Identity First: Every action is a vote for the person you want to become.
  • Never Miss Twice: One miss is human. Two is a new habit.

💡 My Take: Systems beat motivation. This book changed how I approach everything.

How to Talk to Anyone

by Leil Lowndes

★★★★☆

92 practical techniques for better communication — from first impressions to deep connections.

🔑 Key Lessons

  • The Flooding Smile: Pause, then smile. It feels genuine.
  • Sticky Eyes: Maintain eye contact after they finish speaking.
  • Parroting: Repeat their last words as a question.
  • Never the Naked Thank You: Always add a reason.

💡 My Take: Communication is about making others feel seen.
