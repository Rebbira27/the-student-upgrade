TITLE: The Lion and the Mouse
LEVEL: 4
READ_TIME: 6
VOCAB: mighty (adj), scurried (v), tremble (v), courage (n), gnaw (v), savanna (n)
GRAMMAR: Past simple tense for storytelling
Q1: What did the lion learn about strength by the end of the story?
Q2: Find the word "gnaw." What does it mean? What did the mouse gnaw?
Q3: When have you felt underestimated like the mouse? What happened?
CLIFFHANGER: The mouse smiled. "Strength is not only about size, great lion." And from that day, the animals of the savanna told this story to their children...

---
In the highlands of Ethiopia, where the grass grows tall and golden, a mighty lion slept under an acacia tree. His mane spread across the ground like a royal carpet. His breath was slow and deep. He was king of all the animals, and he knew it.
Nearby, a small mouse scurried through the grass, searching for seeds. She was tiny. She was quick. She was invisible to the big creatures of the savanna.
But not invisible enough. She ran straight across the lion's paw.
The lion's eyes snapped open. His massive paw closed around the mouse in an instant. He lifted her to his face, his golden eyes narrowing.
"You disturbed my sleep," the lion growled. "Do you know who I am?"
The mouse trembled but looked the lion in the eyes. "You are the king. Everyone knows this. But please — let me go. One day, I may help you."
The lion laughed. "You? Help me?" His laughter shook the acacia tree. "I could swallow you in one bite."
But the mouse's courage surprised him. He opened his paw and let her fall gently to the ground.
"Go," he said. "Try not to get stepped on."
The mouse bowed deeply and disappeared into the grass.
Weeks passed. The dry season came. The lion hunted alone near the great river, where the ground was soft and the trees were thick with vines.
He did not see the trap.
In one terrible moment, ropes flew up from the ground. A net closed around him and hauled him into the branches. He roared. He clawed. He twisted. But the ropes only tightened. His strength — for the first time in his life — was useless.
His roars echoed across the savanna. Every animal heard. But none came. Why would they? He was the lion. The predator. The one they feared.
But one small creature heard the roaring and recognized the voice. The mouse.
She ran toward the sound, faster than she had ever run. She found the lion hanging in the tree, exhausted and ashamed.
"I am here," she said.
Before the lion could speak, she climbed the tree and began to gnaw at the ropes with her sharp teeth. Rope by rope, strand by strand. The sun set. The moon rose. Still she gnawed. Her jaw ached. Her teeth felt like they would break. But she did not stop.
Just before dawn, the last rope snapped. The lion crashed to the ground.
He lay there, breathing heavily, looking at the tiny mouse who had saved him.
"You were right," he said. "The small can help the strong."
The mouse smiled. "Strength is not only about size, great lion."
From that day forward, the lion and the mouse walked the savanna together. And the other animals learned something new: even the smallest among us has value. Even the weakest has power. Even a mouse can save a king.
